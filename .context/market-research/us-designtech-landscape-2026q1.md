# US DesignTech / PropTech Landscape for Interior Designers (2026 Q1)

**Focus:** Software + hardware integrated products serving interior design workflows
**Scope:** Client acquisition -> scheduling -> site survey (場勘) -> proposal -> project management
**Report Date:** 2026-03-24

---

## Executive Summary

The US market for interior design workflow tools has consolidated significantly in 2024-2025, with key mergers (Ivy absorbed into Houzz Pro, Mydoma merged with Studio Designer). The landscape splits into three layers:

1. **CRM / Client Management Layer** — scheduling, communication, lead tracking
2. **Site Survey / Field Measurement Layer** — LiDAR scanning, 3D capture, floor plans
3. **Project Management / Procurement Layer** — proposals, specs, procurement, invoicing

**No single product covers the entire workflow from client acquisition through site survey to project delivery.** Most firms cobble together 2-3 tools. This represents a significant integration opportunity.

## 5. Emerging Trends & Key Insights (2026)

### 5.1 Consolidation Wave
- **Ivy -> Houzz Pro** (complete absorption)
- **Mydoma + Studio Designer** (merged July 2024, future product strategy unclear)
- **Canvas -> Twindo** (rebrand + platform expansion)
- **Matterport -> CoStar Group** (acquired early 2025, deeper real estate focus)

### 5.2 Phone LiDAR Has Reached "Good Enough" for Interior Design
- iPhone Pro LiDAR achieves ~1% accuracy on room dimensions
- Sufficient for renovation planning, furniture layout, conceptual design
- NOT sufficient for structural engineering or precision construction
- Dramatically lowers the barrier: no hardware purchase needed beyond a phone

### 5.3 AI Integration Points
- **Rendering/visualization:** Rendair AI, Midjourney, ArchiVinci for quick photorealistic previews
- **Project management:** monday.com, ClickUp, Asana adding AI for status updates, delay prediction, client feedback categorization
- **Scan processing:** Polycam, Cupix, NavLive using AI for automatic wall/window/door detection
- **Proposal automation:** Handover PM using AI to transform inquiries into quotations
- **Site capture:** Luma AI for 3D reference models from phone video

### 5.4 The "Stack Gap" — Site Survey Integration
Every major PM platform treats the site survey as something that happens outside their tool. The typical designer workflow in 2026:
1. Get lead via Houzz / Instagram / referral
2. Schedule consultation via HoneyBook / Calendly / LINE
3. Do site survey with tape measure + photos (or Polycam / Twindo / magicplan)
4. Manually re-enter measurements into design software (SketchUp, AutoCAD)
5. Create proposal in separate tool (Programa, Mydoma, or PDF)
6. Manage project in yet another tool

**The integration between steps 3-4-5 is where the most friction exists.** This is the opportunity space.

### 5.5 Hardware + Software Combos Worth Watching

| Combo | What They Do | Why It Matters |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Twindo (Canvas) + iPhone LiDAR** | Scan with phone -> human-verified CAD files in 2 days | Lowest friction scan-to-CAD for small firms |
| **Matterport Pro3 + Matterport Cloud** | Scan -> digital twin -> measurements -> BIM export | Best for client presentations and property marketing |
| **NavLive Radius + Cloud** | Handheld scan -> instant floor plans on device | Fastest field-to-plan, but expensive ($32K) |
| **magicplan + Bluetooth laser meters** | AR floor plans + connected Hilti/Bosch/Leica meters | Best documentation + estimation combo for renovation |
| **Polycam + any LiDAR iPhone** | Quick 3D scan -> floor plans + measurements -> export | Most accessible entry point, strong free tier |
| **Hover + smartphone photos** | Photos -> 3D exterior model + measurements | Exterior-only, but shows the vision for photo-to-model |

## 7. Implications for Q-Check / Interior Javis

### What exists:
- Plenty of tools for **scheduling** (HoneyBook, Dubsado, Houzz Pro CRM)
- Growing ecosystem for **site measurement** (Polycam, Twindo, magicplan)
- Mature tools for **project management** (Programa, Studio Designer, Design Manager)

### What does NOT exist:
- A single platform connecting **site survey data -> risk-aware quotation -> project management**
- Any tool that uses site survey data to **automatically flag risks** in the quotation (e.g., "you measured 30-year-old pipes but didn't include plumbing replacement in your quote")
- Integration between **field measurement tools and quotation/estimation systems** with domain-specific intelligence

### The Q-Check Opportunity:
If Q-Check expands beyond quotation risk-checking to include site condition capture (even lightweight photo/checklist-based, not full LiDAR), it would occupy a unique position: **the only tool connecting site reality to quotation integrity**. This is the gap that no US competitor has filled either.
